UAE Probation Period Rules 2026: Notice Periods Explained (Article 9)

→ Open the UAE Probation Period Calculator

Probation in the UAE has one of the more nuanced notice-period structures in the Gulf — the required notice actually depends on who is ending the employment and why, not just a single flat number.

The 6-month cap

Under Article 9 of Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021, probation cannot exceed 6 months from your actual start date. It can't be extended, and the same employer can't put you on probation twice.

Three different notice periods

Ending employment during probation isn't one-size-fits-all:

  • Employer ends it: at least 14 days' written notice.
  • You resign to join another employer inside the UAE: at least 30 days' (1 month) notice — the longer period exists because your new employer may need to compensate your current employer for recruitment and contract costs.
  • You resign to leave your job entirely (not moving to another UAE employer) or leave the country: at least 14 days' notice, same as the employer-initiated case.

If either side fails to give the required notice, they owe the other party wages for the notice shortfall.

No gratuity during probation

End-of-service gratuity only starts accruing once you've completed 1 full year of continuous service (Article 51) — see our UAE Gratuity Calculator. Since probation is capped at 6 months, a probation-stage termination never reaches that threshold on its own; but the probation months DO count toward your first year if your employment continues afterward.

Worked example

AED 8,000/month salary, resigning during probation to join a new UAE employer: daily wage = 8,000 ÷ 30 = AED 266.7. 30 days' notice pay (if paid in lieu instead of working it) = 30 × 266.7 = AED 8,000.

Frequently asked questions

How long can probation last in the UAE?

Up to 6 months from your actual start date. It cannot be extended, and you cannot be placed on probation twice by the same employer.

How much notice do I need to give if I resign during UAE probation?

It depends: 30 days' notice if you're joining another employer inside the UAE, or 14 days' notice if you're leaving your job entirely (not joining another UAE employer) — under Article 9 of Federal Decree-Law No. 33/2021.

How much notice does my employer need to give to end my probation?

At least 14 days' written notice, the same as if you were resigning to leave the country.

Do I get gratuity if I'm let go during UAE probation?

No — end-of-service gratuity under UAE law only starts accruing after you complete 1 full year of continuous service (Article 51). A probation period (max 6 months) never reaches that threshold on its own.

What happens if my employer doesn't give me proper notice?

Whichever party fails to give the required notice owes the other side wages for the shortfall in the notice period.
