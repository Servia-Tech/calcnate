Oman Gratuity: FAQs, Mistakes & Scenarios

The questions employees ask most about Oman's new-law gratuity — and the mistakes that most often produce a wrong figure.

→ Open the Oman Gratuity Calculator

Oman's 2023 Labour Law changed the gratuity rate, and the transition is where most errors happen. Here are the mistakes to avoid and a set of scenarios that cover the common cases.

5 mistakes people make

1. Applying the new rate to old service

The generous one-month-per-year rate applies to service from August 2023. Service before then keeps the old formula — 15 days per year for the first three years, then one month per year. Applying the new rate to everything overstates the figure.

2. Using gross salary instead of basic

Gratuity is based on basic salary only. Housing, transport and other allowances are excluded.

3. Forgetting the one-year minimum

You need at least one year of continuous service before any gratuity is due.

4. Mishandling the first three old-law years

Under the old formula, the first three years earn only 15 days each, not a full month. This is the single most common miscalculation for pre-2023 service.

5. Ignoring the split entirely

Long-serving staff almost always have service on both sides of August 2023. The calculation must add an old-law portion and a new-law portion.

The full settlement

Gratuity sits alongside your unused leave encashment (paid on gross wage at end of service) and any notice pay. Compare Oman's overall exit terms across the Gulf in the GCC end-of-service comparison.

Reading older guidance carefully

A lot of online material still describes Oman gratuity under the old Royal Decree 35/2003, or blends the two eras. When you read any guide, check that it reflects Royal Decree 53/2023 and that it treats pre- and post-August-2023 service separately. Guidance that quotes a single flat rate for all service is almost certainly out of date or oversimplified.

What counts as service

Your continuous length of service drives the calculation, and a completed probation period counts toward it. So a worker who passed a three-month probation and then worked does not lose those probation months — they are part of the total service that feeds gratuity. This is a helpful protection when you are close to a year-boundary threshold.

Basic vs comprehensive across Oman's exit items

Oman uses different wage bases across its end-of-service lines, which is a frequent source of confusion. Gratuity uses basic salary; notice pay and end-of-service leave encashment use the comprehensive (gross) wage. Applying the wrong base to the wrong line is the most common settlement error.

Putting the settlement together

For your full exit total, add gratuity (basic, split at August 2023), leave encashment (comprehensive wage) and any notice pay (comprehensive wage). Model each on its calculator, and read the complete gratuity guide for the underlying rule behind these answers.

Glossary

Royal Decree 53/2023 — Oman's new Labour Law, which raised the gratuity rate for service from August 2023. Basic salary — the base pay figure used for gratuity, excluding allowances. The split — dividing your service into pre- and post-August-2023 portions, each valued by its own rate. Day of basic — basic ÷ 30, used for the 15-day old-law portions.

The bottom line

Check any guide reflects Royal Decree 53/2023 and treats pre/post-August-2023 service separately. Gratuity uses basic salary; notice and end-of-service leave use the comprehensive wage. A completed probation counts toward the service that drives gratuity.

What you'll need to run the numbers

For an Oman gratuity calculation you need your basic salary (not gross), your total service, and the number of years that fell before August 2023. That split is the single most important input, because pre- and post-August-2023 service are valued differently. Your contract and salary certificate provide the basic figure and the dates.

When to get professional advice

Consider advice where the exact date of your changeover service is disputed, where a package is heavily weighted to allowances (only basic counts), or where continuity of service across related employers is unclear. For most workers, splitting the service and running it through the calculator produces a clean, defensible figure.

Frequently asked questions

Does the new one-month gratuity rate apply to my old Oman service?

No. The one-month-per-year rate applies to service from August 2023. Earlier service keeps the old formula: 15 days per year for the first three years, then one month per year.

Is Oman gratuity calculated on basic or gross salary?

Basic salary only — allowances are excluded.

What is the minimum service to receive gratuity in Oman?

One year of continuous service.

How are the first three years of old-law service treated?

At 15 days per year, not a full month — this is the most common miscalculation for pre-2023 service.
